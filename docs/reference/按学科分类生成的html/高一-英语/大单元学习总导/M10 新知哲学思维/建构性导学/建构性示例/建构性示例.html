真实建构情境

你是某中学高一年级的学生。学校英语角正在筹备一期以 "What Does Friendship Mean to You?" 为主题的英文墙报展示活动。你需要提交一份 Friendship Creed（友谊信条）——一段用英文书写的、属于你自己的、关于友谊的个性化宣言。

你刚刚学完课文 Anne's Best Friend。安妮·弗兰克在二战躲藏期间，把日记本当作自己最亲密的朋友。她的故事让你开始重新思考：朋友究竟意味着什么？

你决定以安妮的故事为起点，结合自己的真实经历，完成一次"从输入到输出"的完整建构。

建构路线总览

跟随虚拟学生 Xiao Ming，看他是如何从课文出发，一步步写出自己的 Friendship Creed 的。

观点形成

Xiao Ming 提炼出 4 句核心观点句：

💡 核心句草案

1. Friendship is not about how often you meet, but about how deeply you understand each other.
2. Anne Frank taught me that a friend can be a diary — someone who never judges and always listens.
3. A real friend is someone who talks back, who challenges you, and who grows with you.
4. True friendship gives you the courage to face the world.

5

语篇建构

连句成篇，运用单元词汇，写成完整 Friendship Creed：

📄 初稿（198词）

My Friendship Creed
Inspired by Anne Frank & My Own Journey

What is friendship? Reading Anne Frank's story made me think about this question seriously.

Anne hid away from the Nazis and went through two years of loneliness. She set down her thoughts in her diary and called it her best friend — a friend whom she could trust completely. From Anne, I learned that friendship is first of all about trust and honesty.

However, my own experience has added another layer. My best friend Li Wei and I are in different schools now, but we still share our ups and downs. Unlike Anne's diary, he talks back — he challenges me, encourages me, and helps me grow.

So here is my creed: A true friend is someone who listens without judging, and speaks without hurting. Friendship is a safe space where you can be yourself — whether that space is a diary, a person, or a memory.

✅ hide away ✅ go through ✅ set down ✅ crazy ✅ 定语从句 (whom)

精简优化 — 终稿

Xiao Ming 将原文压缩至 176 词，得到最终版本：

🌟 终稿 · 176 words

My Friendship Creed

What is friendship? Reading Anne Frank's story made me think deeply about this question.

Anne hid away from the Nazis and went through two years of loneliness. She set down her thoughts in her diary and called it her best friend — a friend whom she could trust completely. From Anne, I learned that friendship begins with trust and honest listening.

However, my own experience has taught me something more. My best friend Li Wei and I are in different schools, but we still share our joys and worries. Unlike Anne's diary, he talks back — he challenges me, encourages me, and helps me grow.

So here is my creed: A true friend is someone who listens without judging and speaks without hurting. Friendship is a safe space where you can be yourself — whether that space is a diary, a person, or a memory in your heart. As Anne wrote, some things must be experienced — and friendship is one of them.
