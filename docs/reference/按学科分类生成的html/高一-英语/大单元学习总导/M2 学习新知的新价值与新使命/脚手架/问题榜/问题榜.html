基础层 · 理解与识别
理解

课文"Anne's Best Friend"中，Anne 为什么把日记当作朋友？

💡 从课文第一段寻找线索——注意"Kitty"这个名字的寓意。

思考路径：当时Anne和家人躲藏在密室里，不能与外界接触，也没有真正的朋友可以倾诉。日记成为了她唯一可以分享内心感受的对象，她把日记命名为"Kitty"，当作一个忠实的朋友来对话。
识别

本单元词汇中，哪些词是描述正面情感的？哪些是负面情感的？

💡 尝试列出至少5个正面词汇和5个负面词汇。

正面：happy, joyful, grateful, hopeful, excited, loving, peaceful
负面：lonely, anxious, depressed, upset, worried, angry, fearful
应用

请从课文中找出3个直接引语的例子，并转换为间接引语。

💡 注意直接引语使用引号，间接引语需要调整人称和时态。

示例：
直接: Anne said, "I don't want to set down a series of facts."
间接: Anne said that she didn't want to set down a series of facts.

进阶层 · 分析与应用
开放

How do you think Anne's situation changed her understanding of friendship?

💡 思考：一个人在失去了正常社交后，对"朋友"的定义会如何变化？

回答框架：Before hiding, Anne had human friends. After going into hiding... (consider: isolation, loneliness, her diary as a substitute, her longing for true connection)
对比

Compare Anne's idea of friendship with your own. What's similar and different?

💡 可以从"朋友的形式""朋友的功能""友谊的基础"三个维度对比。

对比维度：
Similarities: both value trust and sharing feelings
Differences: Anne's "friend" was a diary; modern friendships are often mediated by technology
应用

If you were Anne, what would you write in your diary?

💡 设身处地——想象你被困在一个小房间里两年多，不能出门，你会记录什么？

可写的角度：your daily feelings · descriptions of the people you're with · hopes for the future · fears and anxieties · small joys (a ray of sunshine, a bird's song)

挑战层 · 创造与批判
反事实

If Anne had had a smartphone and internet access in the hiding place, would her diary still have been her best friend? Why or why not?

💡 这是历史与科技的交锋——技术会改变友谊的本质吗？

正反视角：
Yes — because she could connect with real friends online, reducing reliance on a diary
No — because the diary represented a unique private space that even social media can't replace
元认知

When you read about Anne's story, what made you feel empathy? How did you arrive at your interpretation of her feelings?

💡 反思自己的阅读过程——你是如何理解Anne的心情的？什么细节触动了你？

元认知引导：1. Which specific sentence or image triggered your emotion? 2. Did you connect it to any personal experience? 3. How did your initial understanding change as you read more?
批判

The text says Anne's diary became her best friend. Do you think this is a healthy coping mechanism? Why or why not?

💡 从心理学角度思考：把情感完全寄托在一个非人类对象上是健康的吗？

批判性分析：Arguments for healthy: journaling is a recognized therapeutic tool, self-expression prevents emotional suppression. Arguments against: it may deepen isolation if it replaces human connection entirely. Consider the context — in Anne's extreme situation, it was likely the healthiest option available.
创造

Design a modern equivalent of Anne's "Kitty" — what would a teenager today use as their "best friend" to express their feelings?
