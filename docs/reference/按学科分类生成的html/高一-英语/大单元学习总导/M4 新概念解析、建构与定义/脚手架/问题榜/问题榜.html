🎯 问题榜设计理念

以 "问题驱动学习路径" 为核心理念，围绕 Friendship 主题和 M4 "新概念解析、建构与定义" 设计三层递进式问题体系。覆盖 开放性问题、对比性问题、反事实问题和元认知问题 四种类型，兼顾语言表达训练与高阶思维培养。

开放性问题 对比性问题 反事实问题 元认知问题

📋 三层选答

🎯 目标：识别友谊核心词汇、定义和基本特征，建立概念认知起点

Q1开放

What is a "friend"? Try to define the word "friend" in your own words, using at least three English sentences.

Q2开放

List five qualities that you think are the most important in a good friend. Why did you choose each one?

Q3开放

What kinds of activities do you usually do with your friends? Share two or three examples.

Q4对比

What is the difference between a "classmate" and a "friend"? Can a classmate become a friend? Use examples to explain.

Q5对比

Compare "a friend you see every day" and "a friend you only talk to online." What is similar and what is different about these two types of relationships?

Q6反事实

Imagine you had no friends at all. How would your daily life be different? How would you feel?

Q7反事实

What if Anne Frank (from the textbook reading) had not written her diary? Would "Kitty" still be her best friend? Why or why not?

Q8元认知

When you hear the word "friendship," what is the first image or memory that comes to your mind? Why do you think that particular memory appears first?

Q9元认知

Are you good at making new friends? How do you know? What makes you think so?

🎯 目标：分析友谊本质特征、价值冲突，在真实语境中运用语言表达观点

Q10开放

Can a person be happy without any friends? Explain your opinion with reasons and examples from your own experience or from what you have read.

Q11开放

In your opinion, what is the most important thing that keeps a friendship strong over a long period of time? Is it trust, honesty, shared interests, or something else? Justify your choice.

Q12对比

Anne Frank called her diary "Kitty" and treated it as her best friend. Compare Anne's relationship with her diary to a typical friendship between two real people. What are the similarities and differences?

Q13对比

Compare Aristotle's three types of friendship (utility, pleasure, virtue) with the modern definition. Which type is most common among high school students today? Which type lasts the longest?

Q14对比

In Eastern cultures, friendship is often described as "guanxi" (关系) — a network of mutual obligation. In Western cultures, friendship is often seen as a voluntary, personal bond. Compare these two. Which resonates more with your own experience?

Q15反事实

Imagine you discover that your best friend has been spreading a rumor about you. What would you do? Would the friendship survive? Write a short dialogue showing how you would handle this situation.

Q16反事实

What if social media suddenly disappeared tomorrow? How would your friendships change? Would some friendships become stronger or weaker? Explain.

Q17反事实

Imagine you and your best friend move to different cities after high school. What actions would you take to keep the friendship alive? Do you believe long-distance friendships can work? Why or why not?

Q18元认知

Think about a time when you resolved a disagreement with a friend. How did you go about it? What strategy did you use, and would you use the same strategy again?

Q19元认知

When you read Anne Frank's diary excerpt in the textbook, what was the most difficult part for you to understand — the language, the historical context, or the emotions? How did you work through that difficulty?

🎯 目标：从批判性视角审视友谊的社会、文化和伦理维度，创造性提出见解

Q20开放

Can artificial intelligence (AI) — such as a chatbot or a virtual assistant — ever be considered a "friend"? Develop a structured argument (at least 200 words) for or against this idea.

Q21开放

Design your own "Friendship Bill of Rights" — a list of 5-10 rights that every person should have in a friendship. Explain why each right is important.

Q22对比

"A friend in need is a friend indeed" vs "a friend is someone who knows all about you and still loves you." Compare these two definitions. Which captures the deeper essence of friendship?

Q23对比

Compare friendship in the digital age (social media, texting, online gaming) with friendship in the pre-digital era (letters, face-to-face visits, telephone calls). Which era produced "deeper" friendships? What has been gained and what has been lost?

Q24反事实

Imagine a world where everyone could read each other's thoughts. Would friendship still exist? Would it be easier or harder to form deep friendships? Explore this thought experiment in 200-250 words.

Q25反事实

What if your best friend made a serious moral mistake (cheating, stealing, bullying) and asked you to keep it secret. Explore the conflict between friendship loyalty and moral responsibility. Is there a "right" answer?

Q26反事实

What if friendship could be scientifically measured, with each person receiving a "friendship score"? Would this improve human relationships or damage them? Design a brief argument.

Q27元认知

After exploring the questions at all three levels, reflect: Has your personal definition of "friendship" changed or expanded? In what way? What question made you think the hardest, and why?

Q28元认知

When you answered the counterfactual questions (the "What if..." questions), what strategy did you use? Did you imagine yourself in the situation, or did you reason from general principles? Which approach do you find more effective?

💡 使用建议

  • 三层选答机制：根据英语水平和学习目标选择一层作答。基础层保障最低标准，进阶层深化理解，挑战层面向学有余力的学生
  • 课堂融合：解析环节侧重基础/进阶层问题，建构环节侧重进阶/挑战层问题
  • 口头与书面结合：基础层适合课堂口语讨论（pair work / group discussion），进阶层适合小组辩论，挑战层适合书面写作
  • 元认知反思：建议完成某一层问题后优先回答该层元认知问题，培养自主学习意识
  • 积分激励：基础层每题 1 分，进阶层 2 分，挑战层 3 分，激励挑战更高层次
