📖 分层观看任务

面向全体学生 · 视频：The Science of Friendship (SciShow Psych)

👁️ 观看任务

完成"友谊事实记录表"（Facts Graphic Organizer），列出至少 5 个关于友谊的科学研究发现（如大脑中与友谊相关的区域、友谊对健康的积极影响等）。

❓ 关键问题

1. According to the video, what happens in our brain when we interact with a close friend?
2. Why does the video claim that friendship is "a biological necessity, not just a social choice"?
3. What three qualities are identified as the foundation of strong friendships?
[02:15] 视频提到镜像神经元的作用。暂停并思考：Can you recall a moment when you "mirrored" a friend's emotion? Write a one-sentence example.
[04:30] 视频展示友谊与寿命的正相关数据。暂停并思考：Why do you think having close friends can help people live longer?

📝 输出任务（看后产出）

写一段 80-100 词的段落，用视频中的科学证据回答：Why is friendship important for human beings?

完成"友谊事实记录表"并提交。

面向中等及以上学生 · 视频：What Makes a Good Friend? + The Secret to Making Friends as an Adult

👁️ 观看任务

对比两个视频对"什么是好朋友"的定义，完成 Venn Diagram（两个视频观点的交集与差异）。

❓ 关键问题

1. TED-Ed 提出友谊的"三大支柱"（virtue, pleasure, utility）。Which of these three do you think is the most important for a long-term friendship? Justify your answer.
2. In the TEDx talk, the speaker says "friendship is a skill, not a gift." What does she mean by this? Do you agree?
3. 两个视频对"主动建立友谊"的态度有何不同？Which perspective resonates more with your own experience?
[TED-Ed 01:50] Aristotle 的三种友谊分类刚介绍完。暂停并思考：Think of one friend in your life for each category. Do any friendships fall into more than one? Explain briefly.
[TEDx 06:10] 演讲者提到"friendship requires intentional vulnerability"。暂停并思考：What does "intentional vulnerability" look like in a real conversation? Describe a specific scenario.

📝 输出任务（看后产出）

撰写一篇 150-200 词的短文（或录制 2 分钟英语口语视频），题目：My Definition of a True Friend — Insights from Two Videos。要求引用至少两个视频中的观点，并结合个人经历。

完成 Venn Diagram 对比图。

面向学有余力学生 · 视频：Beware of Online "Friendship" + How Social Media Affects Our Relationships

👁️ 观看任务

完成"数字友谊利弊清单"（Pro/Con List），从 真实性（authenticity）、深度（depth）、可持续性（sustainability） 三个维度进行对比评估。

❓ 关键问题

1. 两个视频对"社交媒体上的友谊是否真实"持不同态度。What is each speaker's central argument? Which one do you find more convincing, and why?
2. 纪录片提到"algorithm-driven friendship"这一概念。Do you think an algorithm can truly understand human friendship? Why or why not?
3. 综合两个视频，提出你自己的观点：Can online friendships be as meaningful as offline ones? 你的答案是什么？你需要什么证据来支持它？
[纪录片 03:40] 画面展示社交媒体"好友"数量与实际亲密好友数量的巨大差距。暂停并思考：Look at your own social media (or imagine one). How many "friends" do you have vs. how many people you could truly confide in? What does this gap tell you?
[TED Talk 08:20] 演讲者展示研究：面对面交流每减少 30 分钟，孤独感指数上升 X%。暂停并思考：When was the last time you spent 30 minutes talking face-to-face with a friend, without any phones? How did it feel?

📝 输出任务（看后产出，二选一）

Option A（辩论稿）：撰写一篇 250-300 词 的辩论陈词（Debate Speech），正反立场自选，题目：This House believes that online friendships are as valuable as offline friendships. 要求引用至少三处视频中的证据。

Option B（调研报告）：在班级内完成微型调查（5-10 位同学），将数据与视频观点结合，写一份简短的调研分析报告（200-250 词）。

✅ 课堂实施建议

  • 差异化安排：在 M4 视频环节采用"三层选看"模式，根据英语水平和兴趣选择一层
  • 时间预算：每层视频观看 + 暂停思考约 15-20 分钟，输出任务课后完成或下一课时分享
  • 语言支持：基础层提供英文字幕+关键词汇表；进阶和挑战层仅提供英文字幕；所有层均不提供中文字幕
  • 成果展评：优秀输出作品可在班级展示墙或线上平台分享，计入单元过程性评价
